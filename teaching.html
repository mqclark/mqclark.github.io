Instructor of Record · Bucknell University

Cellular and Molecular Neurobiology (NEUR 253 / BIOL 375)

Explores the major areas of cellular and molecular neurobiology, focusing on major concepts and recent advances in experimental neuroscience: excitable cells and membranes, ion channels and receptors, synaptic transmission, cell-type determination, axon guidance, neuronal cell biology, synapse formation, and homeostasis. The required co-requisite laboratory provides hands-on experience with live animal models as well as virtual explorations of brain organization and function.

Course-based Undergraduate Research Experience — CURE (BIOL 202)

A discovery-based course designed to engage, train, and mentor students in an active-learning CURE that gives them opportunities to gain curiosity-driven research experience (become a scientist), learn to construct and test a hypothesis (think like a scientist), integrate novel findings with known data (work as a scientist), and build fluency in the growing field of neuroscience.

Neural Circuits (BIOL 319)

From the roundworm C. elegans with its 302 neurons to humans with their billions, brains are incredibly complex. We study model systems to understand the general principles of brain function — neurons as information-processing units; simple and complex circuits underlying sensory processing and motor control; and the development and plasticity of neural circuits. Discussion of the primary literature is the main emphasis: small groups select a preprint, review it, and communicate their analysis with the authors, whom we invite to discuss their findings and their scientific careers.
