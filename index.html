What we are asking

Three ways into the problem.

1

Which neurons turn sensory signals into movement?

We examine descending pathways that connect the brain to motor circuits and help shape locomotor choices.

2

How does development assemble a working circuit?

We ask how cells acquire the identities and connections needed for coordinated behavior.

3

What can a fly reveal about behavior more broadly?

Compact nervous systems let us connect genes, anatomy, and action without losing sight of the animal.

Research is a practice

Students do not just watch discovery happen.

In the Clark Lab and in course-based research experiences, students learn to ask tractable questions, design experiments, interpret uncertainty, and share what they learn.
