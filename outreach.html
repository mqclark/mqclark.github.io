Outreach + Creative Work

Science grows by being shared.

Research changes when more people can enter it, question it, picture it, and make it their own.

Pueblo Brain Science

Hands-on neuroscience built through partnership.

Pueblo Brain Science brings together students, educators, researchers, and community partners for hands-on neuroscience workshops at Indigenous-serving institutions in New Mexico.

The work is grounded in collaboration: people bring different forms of knowledge, and the goal is not simply to deliver science, but to create room for new questions, new relationships, and new futures in neuroscience.

✦

Make the invisible visible

Microscopy, illustration, diagrams, and visual narrative can help people see the forms and forces that make behavior possible.

✺

Invite real participation

Hands-on workshops make room for learners to build, test, ask, and revise rather than simply receive information.

✹

Carry research beyond the lab

Public science works best when it is sustained by trust, reciprocity, and respect for the expertise already present in a community.

Creative work

Evidence needs a form people can feel.

Visual storytelling can make scientific process more legible without making it smaller. This space will grow to include microscopy, illustration, documentary work, and other projects that connect scientific detail to a wider public imagination.
