People

Matthew Q. Clark, PhD

Matthew is an Assistant Professor of Biology at Bucknell University, where his lab investigates the neural basis of locomotor behavior in Drosophila. He trained in biology and neuroscience at the University of Oregon and completed postdoctoral work at the California Institute of Technology.

His research follows a throughline: use experimentally tractable nervous systems to understand how neural circuitry produces coordinated behavior — from the interneurons that shape larval crawling to the motor circuits that steer flight.

At Bucknell he brings that work together with undergraduate research and public-facing science. The lab is a place to take questions seriously, make experiments visible, and help students grow into scientists with their own voice.
