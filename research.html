Research

The neurons that make an animal move.

We use the fruit fly to figure out how neural circuits are built during development, and how they turn brain activity into coordinated movement.

Start with the behavior, work back to the circuit.

When an animal moves, its nervous system is choosing an action and timing the muscles to carry it out. In a fly, we can watch a behavior and then trace it back to the specific neurons responsible, which is much harder to do in a bigger brain.

01

Descending neurons

The link between brain and body

Descending neurons carry commands from the brain down to the motor circuits in the nerve cord. One of them, which we helped identify, makes a larva stop crawling forward and back up instead. We're working out how these neurons do that, and how they get wired during development.

02

Circuit development

Building a circuit before it's used

A motor circuit has to be put together correctly before the animal can move at all. We use fly genetics and imaging to follow how neurons take on their jobs and connect to the right partners as the nervous system develops.

03

Methods

How we work

We combine fly genetics, optogenetics, confocal microscopy, and behavioral tracking. No single method gets you there; the aim is always to connect one identified cell type to something the animal actually does.
