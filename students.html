Students + Teaching

Students run the experiments.

You don't have to be a senior with lab experience to do research here. In my courses and in the lab, students take on real projects and make the calls about where they go next.

BIOL 202

A first-year course that runs on real experiments.

BIOL 202 is a course-based research experience, which means the whole class works on questions that don't have known answers yet. Students use optogenetics, switching neurons on and off with light, to test what descending neurons do in fly larvae. They write proposals, run the experiments, critique each other's work, and deal with data that doesn't always cooperate.

It isn't a simulation of research; it's the real thing, at first-year scale.

Mentorship

What mentorship looks like here.

I give direct, specific feedback, and I expect a lot, while also expecting it to take time to get there. The goal is for you to leave more independent than you arrived: able to run your own experiments, defend your own conclusions, and work well with the people around you.
