About the lab.

Matthew Q. Clark is an Assistant Professor of Biology at Bucknell University. His lab studies how the nervous system produces movement, using the fruit fly Drosophila.

Matthew Q. Clark, PhD

A bit of background.

Matthew started college at Pima Community College in Tucson and finished his undergraduate degree at the University of Arizona. He did his PhD at the University of Oregon in Chris Doe's lab, studying the circuits that drive crawling in fly larvae, and a postdoc at Caltech in Michael Dickinson's lab, working on flight.

He joined Bucknell in 2020. The lab keeps a foot in both research and teaching, and much of its work runs through a first-year course-based research experience. Matthew also co-leads Pueblo Brain Science, which brings hands-on neuroscience to communities in New Mexico.
